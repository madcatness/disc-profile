DISC Colour Profiles

Traits · Body language · Working styles · Stress · Feedback

Dominance · Extrovert · Task

Red

Focus on what  ·  Anger: shot glass (fast, full, resets)

Strengths
  • Natural leader — decisive, driven, takes initiative
  • Competitive, results-oriented, sets high standards on themselves and others
  • Persuasive and strong-willed, gets through any obstacle
  • Time-conscious, doesn't care what others think
Weaknesses
  • Control freak, may trample others
  • Short-tempered, arrogant, impatient
  • Happy to break rules or take shortcuts if the job gets done
  • Brutally blunt, not afraid of conflict
Body language
  • Strong handshake, direct eye contact, forward lean
  • Brisk walk, controlling gestures including pointing
  • Loud, fast-paced voice, not afraid to raise it
Written comms style

Blunt, short, to the point

Stress triggers
Losing control No results Routine Wasted time Stupid mistakes
Under stress
  • Blames everyone else, shuts down, works even harder
  • Doesn't consider themselves angry — yelling is just communication
  • May miss feedback because of outbursts
Working with Red
  • Do what you're told quickly, speak up, reduce small talk, stick to topic
  • Be direct, give your opinion, stand up for yourself, show commitment
  • Explain that keeping an eye on details creates better outcomes
  • Point out the risks of hurrying — mistakes, others not keeping up
  • Remind them they need others and that relationships matter
  • Confront poor behaviour or bullying directly
  • To help under stress: send them home to burn energy or exercise
Feedback & bad news
  • No gift-wrapping, stick to concrete facts, don't worry about emotions
  • Be prepared for pushback, don't play along
  • Ask them to repeat what you said
Influence · Extrovert · People

Yellow

Focus on who  ·  Anger: glass (gradual build, guilt after)

Strengths
  • Optimistic, enthusiastic, captures attention easily
  • Outgoing, communicative, can talk to anyone
  • Creative, resourceful, inspiring, charming and convincing
  • Quick gut-based decisions, flexible and spontaneous
Weaknesses
  • Talks too much, bad listener, easily distracted and careless
  • Stories may not reflect reality, can seem superficial or selfish
  • Punctuality and admin are not priorities
  • Easily bored, all talk and no follow-through
Body language
  • Touchy-feely, comes close, smiles often
  • Expressive gestures, friendly eye contact
  • Fast speech with lots of vocal variation — mouths can't keep up
Written comms style

Fluffy, expressive, long-winded, spontaneous

Stress triggers
Being ignored Negativity Isolation Strict routine Public humiliation
Under stress
  • Draws even more attention than usual
  • Becomes excessively and unrealistically optimistic
Working with Yellow
  • Create a warm, friendly environment, keep detail low, be open and approachable
  • Follow your gut, bring creativity and excitement to interactions
  • Check they're actually listening — ask questions to confirm
  • Coordinate appointments properly, don't rely on "that won't take long"
  • Help create lists and structure, they tend toward messiness
  • Provide diplomatic motivation to convert talk into action
  • They like to look good and be centre of attention — use this
  • To help under stress: let them organise something fun or social
Feedback & bad news
  • Follow an agenda — they will hijack the conversation
  • Concrete examples, show how it impacts others
  • Explain you dislike the behaviour, not them
  • Make sure they acknowledge what you said, ask them to repeat it
  • Prepare for a strong defence mechanism, follow up as soon as you can
Stability · Introvert · People

Green

Focus on how  ·  Anger: barrel (rare, inward, erupts big)

Strengths
  • Best listener — patient, kind, reliable team player
  • Calm, tolerant, considerate, relationship-focused
  • Stable, predictable, thinks well of most people
Weaknesses
  • Indecisive, wishy-washy, you often won't know where they stand
  • Stays silent to avoid conflict, can seem dishonest
  • Resistant to change, rarely makes the first move
  • Avoids risk and responsibility, can seem gullible or naive
Body language
  • Relaxed, comes close, tends to lean backwards
  • Friendly eye contact, small-scale gestures
  • Soft but warm voice, slower and steadier than Yellow
Written comms style

Soft, warm, personal

Stress triggers
Loss of security Fast change Spotlight Disagreements Redoing work
Under stress
  • Becomes reserved, cold, hesitant and uncertain
  • Refuses to change, digs in and becomes stubborn
  • Anger directed inward — can cause anxiety and burnout over time
Working with Green
  • Driven by security — accept they fear change, be willing to listen
  • Give them periods of space, peace, quiet and inactivity
  • Take care of planning and explain what is happening step by step
  • Be patient with change, break processes into small steps
  • Take command — they won't step up first
  • Always criticise in private, never in front of others
  • To help under stress: allow them to do nothing and rest
Feedback & bad news
  • Use a gentle tone, concrete examples, relational framing
  • Be gentle but don't back-pedal — clearly but softly
  • Address the "I'm so stupid" spiral — it's the behaviour, not the person
  • Ask them to repeat and follow up as soon as possible
Compliance · Introvert · Task

Blue

Focus on why  ·  Anger: barrel with tap (slow build, daily release)

Strengths
  • Analytical, methodical, detail-oriented, accurate
  • Logical, cautious, systematic — thinks carefully before deciding
  • Thorough, reflective, modest, asks great questions
Weaknesses
  • Perfectionist, hesitant to start or finish anything
  • Suspicious, cold, aloof until they know you well
  • Overthinker, obsessed with detail, can be over-critical
  • Delivers feedback in an insensitive, blunt fashion
Body language
  • Keeps distance, often closed body language, direct eye contact
  • Few gestures, little expression, values personal space
  • Controlled, subdued voice — slowest tempo of all colours
Written comms style

Factual, detailed, impersonal

Stress triggers
Being told they're wrong Surprises Spontaneous decisions Emotional people Being pushed to innovate
Under stress
  • Excessive pessimism, lethargy, loss of interest
  • Becomes pedantic and a know-it-all
  • Releases pressure in tiny daily grumbles and complaints
Working with Blue
  • Prepare meticulously — no excuses, they've thought of everything
  • Stick to facts, precise details and realistic goals, never lie
  • Stay focused on the task — they're not here to hang out
  • Values quality — be meticulous if you want to impress
  • Remind them that people have feelings when being critical
  • Calmly tell them when they need to work faster — haste isn't always sloppy
  • Point out that it is logical to use some intuition, not just rational thought
  • Provide information and push for a decision if stagnation occurs
  • To help under stress: give them privacy, space and time
Feedback & bad news
  • Specific, detailed examples — ideally in writing
  • Stick to facts, don't get personal, ask for their suggestions to improve
  • Be ready for deep counter-questions at a molecular level
  • Ask them to repeat and follow up soon after
Group dynamics

Natural pairs: Blue + Green (both cautious, introverted, think before acting) · Red + Yellow (both fast, extroverted, shoot from the hip)

Task pairs: Blue + Red (devoted to work, no small talk needed) · Yellow + Green (one talks, one listens — won't get much done but will enjoy it)

Opposite combos (Yellow + Blue, Red + Green) need extra self-awareness — each person must stretch to meet the other.
